# Research Analyst Skills

## 1. Literature Extraction
When reviewing a method/paper:
1. Identify the core innovation (e.g., "They use a memory bank to store normal features").
2. Evaluate its computational cost (O(N) vs O(N^2)).
3. Assess its reliance on large datasets vs few-shot capabilities.

## 2. InCTRL Adaptation
When proposing a new idea for the pipeline:
1. Map the academic concept to InCTRL components (e.g., "Apply their spatial attention concept to our Visual Adapter").
2. Detail the expected benefits and potential failure modes in an industrial setting.
3. Formulate a specific hypothesis that the team can test.

## 3. Method Comparison
When asked to compare approaches:
1. Create a clear comparison matrix (Pros, Cons, Compute Cost, Data Requirements).
2. Recommend the optimal approach based on the specific constraints of the current dataset (e.g., VisA vs MVTec).
